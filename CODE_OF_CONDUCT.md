# Code of Conduct

This project is committed to a safe, inclusive, and respectful community. We welcome people of every background and identity and expect all contributors and users to help keep our community harassment-free.

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:**
- Being kind, respectful, and empathetic.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project.
- Using inclusive language and actively supporting diverse voices.
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Acknowledging mistakes and apologizing when appropriate.

**Examples of unacceptable behavior include:**
- Harassment, discrimination, or derogatory comments or jokes.
- Threats of violence or violent language.
- Personal insults; trolling or baiting.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Publishing others’ private information (doxing) without explicit consent.
- Disruptive, antagonistic, or off-topic behavior that derails discussions.
- Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code applies to all project spaces and interactions, including but not limited to: GitHub issues, PRs, code reviews, discussions, mailing lists, chat, video calls, conferences, and private communications initiated in the context of the project. It applies to every participant—maintainers, contributors, users, sponsors, and vendors.

## Maintainers’ Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior; taking appropriate and fair corrective action; and communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate. Maintainers must avoid and disclose conflicts of interest and recuse themselves when necessary.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it as soon as possible:

- **Primary contact:** **AUSP59** — **alanursapu@gmail.com**
- **Subject line suggestion:** `[CoC Report] <short summary>`
- **Include (if comfortable):** your contact info, names of people involved, when/where it happened (links or evidence if available), and a brief description of what occurred.

Reports will be handled confidentially. We will acknowledge receipt as soon as possible and generally aim to respond within **3 business days**. If your report involves a maintainer listed above, we will appoint an alternate responder and the named maintainer will be recused.

## Enforcement Process

The response team will:
1. **Acknowledge** receipt and confirm understanding.
2. **Assess** the report, gather context, and evaluate potential risks to the community.
3. **Decide** on actions proportionate to the behavior and history.
4. **Communicate** the outcome to the reporter (and to the reported individual, if appropriate).
5. **Document** actions privately for accountability.

We will maintain confidentiality to the extent possible and are committed to **non-retaliation**: retaliation against anyone who reports an incident or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited.

## Consequences (Enforcement Ladder)

Potential actions include, separately or in combination:
- **Correction:** A private, written warning with guidance on expected behavior.
- **Warning:** A public or private warning; required confirmation of understanding.
- **Temporary Restrictions:** Limited or suspended participation (e.g., commenting, PRs) for a defined period.
- **Temporary Ban:** Removal from community spaces and events for a defined period.
- **Permanent Ban:** Permanent removal from community spaces and contribution channels.

Where appropriate, we may require training, a written apology, or other restorative steps. Severe violations may result in immediate bans.

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was made in error or was disproportionate, you may file an **appeal** by emailing **alanursapu@gmail.com** with the subject `[CoC Appeal]`. Appeals should include any context not previously considered. When feasible, a maintainer not involved in the original decision will review the appeal.

## Public Statements

We will not publicly share private details of a report without the reporter’s consent, except as required by law or when there is an immediate risk of harm. We may publish anonymized summaries to improve transparency.
